Biography

Ruskin Bond, a writer with British roots, was born on May 19, 1934, in Kasauli, located in Himachal Pradesh, India. He is renowned for his stories set in the hills and mountains of India, particularly focusing on the areas of Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand. Bond has authored more than a hundred works, including novels, essays, and short story collections. Among his most well-known books are “The Blue Umbrella,” “A Flight of Pigeons,” “A Handful of Nuts,” and “The Room on the Roof.” His contributions to literature have been recognized with several prestigious awards from the Indian government, such as the Padma Shri and Padma Bhushan. Today, Ruskin Bond continues to write and enchant readers with his evocative storytelling.
